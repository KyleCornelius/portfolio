Health Hub

Kyle Cornelius

Overview

Optum is part of a world wide healthcare organization, UnitedHealth Group. They are the largest single employer of doctors & nurses in the United States. Optum is constantly looking for ways track and monitor patients so they can improve patient health while decrease overall costs through prevention.

Challenge

The Gap. The immense gap in time between doctors visits means most doctors are working on few or no data points when working with a patient.

Goal

Bridge the gap with data. Allowing doctors to see a patients IoT health data allows doctors to get a much better understanding of a patients health.

Instead of doctor tracking your health say, once a year, a doctor can get access to things like: your vitals, blood glucose, nutrition, and stress levels at any time with millions of data points down to the minute.

IoT devices are booming, with billions more becoming active every year

Process

Understanding and defining the user and their needs is the first step in the process. Spending the time to truly understand the problem will help to create the best design later.

Depending on where in the iterative process I am, I could be creating crude wireframes to start to put my ideas onto paper, designing the user flow, prototyping, working on a more detailed solution, or making small tweaks based on feedback. The focus is on functionality and usability. This part of the process is all about the creativity.

Gaining insight from users is a great way to improve your design while eliminating problems and difficulties that were unforeseen in the design phase.

Feedback

During testing we found that users had trouble finding their IoT device to connect it. The process was more cumbersome then initially realized, so the entire process was designed so that users now connected by service instead of device type. This eliminated many of the confusing options and streamlined the progress.
